Previous Projects

SOTERIA is a research project that was presented in the National Science and Technology Fair 2022 under the Robotics and Intelligent Machines category. The project was conducted to primarily to contribute in the 9th Sustainable Development Goal: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure which aims to promote the underlying sector for the continuous growth of the country. The project used arduino and xethru x4m300 uwb radar to equip a rescue bot with an ability to detect signs of life behind large debris; aiding rescuers in a disaster rescue missions.

BIT RUSH is a javaFX-based game project that was developed as part of the course requirements for CMSC 22 of ICS-UPLB. It is a order-delivery game was inspired from food delivery services and from the common themes found in UPLB and nearby areas. BIT RUSH features audio-visual feedbacks, interactive controls, scoring, time-tracking, and pane swapping. Most importantly the development employs OOP-based approach in represent the game concepts and design of the project.

SINAYA is a research project conducted in collaboration of the SPCSIHS students and BFAR-Region 4A in 2023. The project was a Fuzzy Neural Network model that infers possible values for ammonia level using sets of pre-gathered data of pH level, temperature, and dissolved oxygen level in mariculture sites along Batangas Province. SINAYA was developed to promote 14.7 Life Below Water and 2.a Zero Hunger of the Sustainable Development Goals through the use of modern technological and mathematical approach in creating sustainable countries.
